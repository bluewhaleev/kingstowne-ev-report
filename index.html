02 / An Honest Read of the Data

What the survey is really telling us.

Signal: a clear yes from a motivated minority

320 respondents scored 9 or 10 on likelihood of next-EV adoption. 224 already drive plug-in vehicles. These residents are not theoretical customers — they are actively trying to charge vehicles today, often in townhomes without garage access.

The retention signal is especially notable: a Stratford Place resident told us they are "currently considering not renewing my lease for this reason alone."

Signal: a vocal opposition with legitimate concerns

40% of respondents said chargers would not make them more likely to stay. 39.6% voted against retention benefit entirely. Common themes: concerns about guest parking loss, HOA fee increases, non-resident access, and aesthetics.

These concerns are design problems, not deal-breakers. A credible proposal must address each one directly.

05 / Program Economics

Dominion Energy can change the project math.

For eligible Virginia commercial and industrial customers, Dominion Energy's Level 2 Charging Program provides a 50% upfront incentive on EV charging make-ready construction and installation, then lets the customer pay the remaining program costs through monthly on-bill payments.

Why this matters for a Kingstowne pilot

The largest barrier to neighborhood charging is usually the electrical work before a charger is ever installed: trenching, conduit, wiring, panels, and other make-ready infrastructure. The Dominion program directly targets that cost category, which is why a limited pilot can be evaluated as an operating model rather than only as a capital request.

Program structure

50% make-ready incentive Dominion describes the incentive as applying to EV charging construction and installation, commonly referred to as make-ready.
Lower upfront burden The remaining eligible costs can be paid through monthly on-bill payments over the program term, helping avoid a large one-time capital outlay.
Customer control of charging operations Site hosts can set driver pricing and use policy, which lets KROC test cost recovery, access rules, and parking impacts during a pilot.
Eligibility still needs confirmation Participation generally requires a Virginia commercial or industrial Dominion Energy customer, decision authority for the site, at least two qualifying charging stations, a program contract, and site readiness.

Program description summarized from Dominion Energy's public Level 2 Charging Program materials and the provided Blue Whale EV program transcript. Final eligibility, pricing, and contract terms should be confirmed with Dominion Energy before board approval.

06 / Recommendation

A measured path forward.

Proposed next step

Pilot deployment in 3–5 highest-priority neighborhoods.

The data supports moving forward, but not at scale and not all at once. A phased pilot in the highest-scoring neighborhoods lets KROC demonstrate real utilization, refine the operating model, and address design concerns with data rather than projections — all before committing community-wide capital.

I.

Pilot (Year 1)

Install Level-2 chargers at Eton Square, Dunwich Square, and Northampton. These neighborhoods combine highest demand with strongest utilization signals. Electrical make-ready at 2–3 additional sites for future expansion.

II.

Validate (Year 1–2)

Track actual usage, non-resident access, parking impact, and cost recovery. Publish results quarterly to the board and membership. Address the parking and outsider-access concerns raised in this survey head-on, with real data.

III.

Scale or Stop

If utilization matches the survey signal and operational concerns are manageable, expand to the next tier of priority neighborhoods. If not, the pilot contained the risk and the board has data to justify pausing.
